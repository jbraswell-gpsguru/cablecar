San Francisco Cable Car System - Before & After

Background

The cable car system of San Francisco attracts tourists and locals alike every year and generates a lot of revenue for the city. The system was invented by Andrew Halladie and started running on August 2, 1873, replacing horse drawn carriages that couldn't make it to the stop of the steep hills, leaving passengers injured or killed. 23 lines ran throughout the entire city, but gradually decreased to what is currently today 3 lines running within more of the downtown core. The areas the three lines run through today include Chinatown, Fisherman's Wharf, Financial District, North Beach, Russian Hill, Nob Hill, and Union Square. These lines include the Powell-Mason, Powell-Hyde, and the California. There were several factors that led to the decline of many of the lines. One included the 1906 earthquake and fire resulting in many of the powerhouses and barns that stored many of the cars along many of the old lines to be destroyed along with the cars stored within them. This resulted in 8 lines operating by 1912. Another included innovations in new forms of transportation that paved the way for streetcars and buses run in San Francisco.

The Fight To Remain Open

Mayor of San Francisco Roger Lapham proposed to replace the cable car system back then with buses. This outraged many native San Franciscans such as this one woman named Friedel Klussmann. Klussmann led the movement to save the cable cars by forming a petition and raising awareness as to what the mayor's plans were. After garnering a lot of support, the petition created a proposition that landed on the ballot to save the cable car system and was passed by many voters, which resulted in the three lines. The cable cars are a moving landmark to both San Francisco and also the entire nation that attract tourists and locals every year to experience what transportation was like before the automobile was invented.

Current Lines

Pictures of the three cable car lines that are currently running in San Francisco.

Powell & Hyde Line

Powell & Hyde Line runs from Union Square to Aquatic Park by Ghirardelli Square through Russian Hill, past crooked Lombard Street, down the steep Hyde Street Hill.

Powell & Mason Line

Powell & Mason Line runs from Union Square to the heart of Fisherman's Wharf, through Russian Hill and North Beach.

California Line

California Line from Financial District at Market Street to Nob Hill at Van Ness Avenue along California Street, crossing Chinatown at Grant Avenue along the way.

Former Lines

Pictures of many of the former cable car lines that are now replaced by the majority of the bus lines of San Francisco. Some of the lines have multiple branches.

California Street Railway

There was a branch of the California Street line known as the O'Farrell, Jones & Hyde Streets line that ran along part of what is currently the Powell-Line.

Sutter Street Railway

The Sutter Street Railway consisted of both the Pacific Street Line and the Sutter Street Line.

Geary Street, Park and Ocean Railway

The Geary Street, Park and Ocean Railway ran from Union Square to Golden Gate Park near Haight/Ashbury Neighborhood. The MUNI 38 lines run along the route today.

Presidio & Ferries Railway

The Presidio & Ferries Railway ran in between Downtown and the Presidio with stops along the way in North Beach and Russian Hill.

Market Street Railway

The Market Street Railway ran from the waterfront of San Francisco to the eastern neighborhoods. The line originated at the Ferry Building on the Embarcadero and went along Market Street in the heart of the Financial District. After leaving the Financial District, the line started to diverge into seperate branches of lines.

One line went to the northern part of Golden Gate Park where it met with the Geary Street, Park and Ocean Railway Line. The other line went through the heart of Haight/Ashbury neighborhood ending at Stanyan Street at Golden Gate Park. The other two lines went alongside of the Castro District and the Mission District.

Ferries & Cliff House Railway

The Ferries & Cliff House Railway Line originated from the Powell Street Cable Car Turnaround at Powell and Market just a few blocks south of Union Square and by the Powell Street BART/MUNI Station and from the Ferry Building on The Embarcadero. The railway line consisted of two branches that include the Powell Street Lines and the Clay Sacramento Line. The lines ran along where both the two Powell Lines run along to Fisherman's Wharf. The Clay Sacramento Line started from the Ferry Building and went through North Beach and Russian Hill, ending just a few blocks south of the Presidio.

Omnibus Railway & Cable Company

The Omnibus Railway consited of three branches which included the Howard Street Line, Haight & Leavenworth Street Lines, and Ellis Street Line.
